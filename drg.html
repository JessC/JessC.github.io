Trucks

Specifically 3rd generation Tacomas but info applies to most

  • ECT (Electronically Controlled Transmission) - This button is similar to a Sport Mode you would find in a performance car. It causes the transmission to downshift more often and will hold the gear longer before upshifting. This is especially helpful when getting on freeways with short onramps. Read about ECT button

  • Traction Control - even these base model Tacomas can be reasonably capable vehicles if you know how to use them. There's a lot of manipulation happening to the wheels automatically which is good normal driving conditions. When you're driving off road the truck will limit power to the wheels when it senses slippage. Sometimes the ground is so loose that the wheels are need to slip a little. But if the truck is trying to prevent this then you may end up stuck because the truck will refuse to go. Putting the truck in 4WD turns off most of these features. But you can do it in 2WD by hold in TRAC button for 3 seconds while you are at a COMPLETE stop. Pushing the button for less than 3 seconds or while moving will result in assed setting which will turn on Auto LSD and the truck will try to apply the brakes to the wheels that are slipping the most. I'll explain more next.

  • LSD (auto limited slip) - The main problem with a standard differential is that it sends power to the wheel with the least amount of traction. Our trucks are equiped with a FAKE limited slip differential system. Basically the truck will apply the brake to only the wheel thats slipping in hopes that more power is transferred to the other wheel. In my experience this system sucks. Just put the truck in 4 wheel drive and move on with your day.Differentials

  • 4WD - You can activate it while driving. This is helpful if the road suddenly turns to dirt. You can shift into H4 up to speeds just over 60mph. It will let you know if you're going to fast for it to be activated. It's not great for the drivetrain to be in 4WD when the truck is on pavement or when the wheels have sufficient traction. Read about AWD vs 4WD

  • 4Lo - You have to be in Neutral (not Park) and be at a complete stop. Use this when climbing steep dirt access roads or anywhere else you need traction. It's also good for more safely decending sections of dirt road. Put the truck in manual shift mode and drive down in 1st or 2nd gear so you don't have to ride the brake.

  • cabin air filter - This is the filter that cleans the air entering the truck. We drive in situations that might cause the filter two get dirty much sooner than our normal service intervals (oil changes). If you feel like your filter might not be working you can take it out and tap it on the ground or against a tree to shake the dust out to get more life out of it. It's located behind the glove box. You can access it by opening the glove box and opening the little access door. Then filter just slides out.

  • cataliytic converter - These reduce the amount of toxic emmisions that exit you're vehicle through the exhaust pipe. How do they work? Not important, just know they need to get really hot to do their job. They can get as hot as an open flame. And guess where they're located. They're under the truck between the engine and the muffler. If you're working in hot and dry conditions you might want to give the underside of you're truck a quick glance when you park if you think theres a chance you may have picked a dead branch or debris. Shit, I'll sometimes give it a little squirt with the water backpack just to drop the temp down a few hundred degrees if I think a tumble weed or some trash might find it's way under there.

  • VMPI layers - The layers list controls what items are shown on the VMPI map. So if you're SSD, lines, roads, etc are not showing up then you need to look through the layer list and check the box. It's not always obvious by name. Have fun figuring it out. I'll include a cheat sheet to the common items when I have a sec
  • airplane/eco mode - Put your getac in airplane mode and eco mode when you're going to be away from your truck for a while. The battery will last substantially longer. FYI, it will cancel eco mode and return to normal every time you plug it in.
