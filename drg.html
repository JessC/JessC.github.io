Trucks

Specifically 3rd generation Tacomas but info applies to most

  • ECT (Electronically Controlled Transmission) - This button is similar to a Sport Mode you would find in a performance car. It causes the transmission to downshift more often and will hold the gear longer before upshifting. This is especially helpful when getting on freeways with short onramps. Read about ECT button

  • Traction Control - even these base model Tacomas can be reasonably capable vehicles if you know how to use them. There's a lot of manipulation happening to the wheels automatically which is good normal driving conditions. This can be undesirable and even unsafe in off road conditions. When you're driving off road the truck will limit throttle response when it senses a reduction in traction. Sometimes the ground is so loose that the wheels are need to slip a little. But if the truck is trying to prevent this then you may end up stuck because the truck will refuse to go. (I have been parked in mud on flat ground and with the pedal to the floor and the truck refused to give power to the wheels) Putting the truck in 4WD turns off most of these features and will avoid digging ruts and destroying land, use it. In rare cases you might want to turn off the features in 2WD if a sudden loss of power could be dangerous. One example would be pulling out onto a highway from a muddy access road. You can turn off the features in 2WD by holding in the TRAC button (pic below) for 3 seconds while you are at a COMPLETE stop. Pushing the button for less than 3 seconds or while moving will result in some half ass'd setting which will turn on Auto LSD and the truck will try to apply the brakes to the wheels that are slipping the most. See next section.

  • LSD (auto limited slip differentials) - The main problem with a standard differential is that it sends power to the wheel with the least amount of traction. Our trucks are equiped with a FAKE limited slip differential system. Basically the truck will apply the brake to only the wheel thats slipping in hopes that more power is transferred to the other wheel. In my experience this feature sucks. Just put the truck in 4 wheel drive and move on with your day. Differentials

  • 4WD - You can activate it while driving. This is helpful if the road suddenly turns to dirt or you're crossing a creek. You can shift into H4 at speeds up to around 60mph. It will let you know if you're going to fast for it to be activated. So if your driving and traction becomes questionable because of rain, ice, mud, etc then just turn the knob to 4H. It's not great for the drivetrain to be in 4WD when the truck is on pavement or when the wheels have sufficient traction. Read about AWD vs 4WD

  • 4LO - You have to be in Neutral (not Park) and be at a complete stop. Use this when climbing steep dirt access roads or anywhere else you need traction. It's also good for more safely decending sections of dirt road. Put the truck in manual shift mode and drive down in 1st or 2nd gear so you don't have to ride the brake.

  • cabin air filter - This is the filter that cleans the air entering the truck. We drive in situations that might cause the filter two get dirty much sooner than our normal service intervals (oil changes). If you feel like your filter might not be working you can take it out and tap it on the ground or against a tree to shake the dust out to get more life out of it. It's located behind the glove box. You can access it by opening the glove box and popping out the little access door. The filter just slides out.

  • cataliytic converter - These reduce the amount of toxic emmisions that exit you're vehicle through the exhaust pipe. How do they work? Not important, just know they need to get really hot to do their job. They can get as hot as an open flame. And guess where they're located. They're under the truck between the engine and the muffler. If you're working in hot and dry conditions you might want to give the underside of you're truck a quick glance when you park if you think theres a chance you may have picked a dead branch or debris. Shit, I'll sometimes give it a little squirt with the water backpack just to drop the temp down a few hundred degrees if I think a tumble weed or some trash might find it's way under there while I'm away.

    GETAC

  • VMPI layers - The layers list controls what items are shown on the VMPI map. So if you're SSD, lines, roads, etc are not showing up then you need to look through the layers list and check the appropriate box. It's not always obvious by name as seen below. Have fun figuring it out.

  • airplane/eco mode - Put your getac in airplane mode and eco mode when you're going to be away from your truck for a while. The battery will last substantially longer. FYI, it will cancel eco mode and return to normal every time you plug it in.


  • IT store
  • Software Google Chrome - Can be downloaded on your GETAC through the software section of the IT Store. Pretty soon Google Drive will not be supported on IE so might as well get it now. Also, on rare occasions I've been able to open my Davey Gmail on my GETAC with Chrome.
  • Hardware - You can order hardware yourself here. GETAC chargers and batteries are a good example of what you can order. The store will let you know if an item needs authorization. *make sure what you order is PG&E equipment and not DRG. Phone chargers would be an example of non PG&E equipment.

  • physical access - you can request access to building yourself through PGE@Work For Me. Just go to My Physical Access and follow instructions. Tip: If you know you're going to be roaming somewhere it's a good idea to do this ahead of time because it can take a couple days to get approved.

    • Phones

    mostly applys to iPhones.

  • Google Maps - When you're working places without great cell reception it's helpful to use offline maps. Just select the feature from the menu and then select the area that you want to be available offline. It will download that section of the map to your phone.


  • Scanning documents - There's a lot of apps that can be used to scan documents with your phone. These are three of my favorite and why.
    • Adobe Scan - The app works really well and when it senses the document it automatically captures it. This is nice since having to push a capture button can cause the camera to move. It also connects nicely to the DRG Google Drive
    • Office Lens - It's Microsofts version. I like it because it can capture documents as photos and they can get stored directly to your photo album where they can be shared easily
    • Notes app - It's a native Apple App so it's already on your phone and one less thing to download
  • Tree ID
    • - There are apps that can use your phones camera to help you identify tree species. I don't know what the best one is but I use iNaturalist. If you want to know more infor mation about a particular tree species try here: https://selectree.calpoly.edu/
